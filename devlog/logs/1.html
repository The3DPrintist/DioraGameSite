Let's Get Caught Up

8/20/2023

Hello World!

During the 2023 Playdate Developer Lunch, Neven Mrgan told me I should make a devlog for Diora. I am finally ready, and I hope this will be interesting to devs as well as people interested in Diora. Since it is almost exactly a year since I started the project, I'd like to cover a brief history of Diora up to the present.

Which one is me? Guess you had to be there.


In 2022 while working on Voyage (rip) and Pool, I had imagined a game that consisted of exploring small worlds contained within jars. This original idea has morphed over time into what Diora is now, but some aspects of it are still there. I imagined these worlds on a 3D grid made of blocks, where you can rotate the environment using the Playdate crank. The idea was inspired by many games I enjoyed, Including Monument Valley and Toad's Treasure Tracker. Some art and design aspects came from these games that I enjoyed. This idea stayed with me but I couldn't wrap my head around how it would be possible on the Playdate. I was content in thinking that it was impossible, or at least too resource intensive to run on Playdate hardware.

I had my aha moment almost exactly a year ago, on 8/18/2022. I don't remember where I was when I had my aha moment but I was probably at my computer. When I did, I was quickly able to convince myself it would be possible to pull off, finally realizing how to handle the rendering and data. I was excited and spent 4 days programming the rendering logic and a rudimentary editor, and made my first post of Diora's engine.

First (public) Diora Footage (8/22/2022)


Diora uses pre-rendered blocks stored in large image tables, which are rendered using a list of blocks. This list is sorted by distance of each block to the camera. This means the blocks can be drawn in order of the list to avoid a depth buffer. The blocks are drawn at the correct point on screen, using a custom algorithm I made to be as fast as possible. Don't worry, I will cover the rendering engine in more detail in a future log.

I was really excited that it worked, however, it barely ran at 30 FPS on the Playdate with around 20 blocks. I have spent a large amount of development time on optimizations, allowing for the game to run at a much better FPS with much higher block counts. Optimizations will also be expanded on in a future log.


I have spent the last year working hard on Diora whenever I can, and I am making good progress. No release window to announce just yet, I am a full time college student and intern after all. I hope these are interesting, and thank you to everyone who supports me, It's great to be a part of this community, really.
